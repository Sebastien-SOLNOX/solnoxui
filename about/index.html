About Solnox Systems

Technology work with a steady hand and a coastal sense of space.

Solnox Systems exists for teams that want thoughtful digital capability without unnecessary complication. We bring structure, taste, and technical judgment to projects that need to move well.

Our point of view

Good systems should feel easy to live with.

We believe technology should clarify work, not add another layer of friction. The best products and systems are understandable, adaptable, and built close to the actual needs of the people using them.

That means listening carefully, designing with restraint, and choosing tools for their usefulness rather than their novelty.

Considered

We slow down just enough to make the right work obvious.

Useful

Every decision should make the product, process, or business easier to operate.

Maintainable

We care about what happens after launch, when real teams have to keep moving.

Core team

Founded by people who care about resilient communities.

Solnox Systems is led by two co-founders with a shared respect for Australia, New Zealand, and the communities working toward cleaner, more independent ways of living.

Co-founder and CTO

Sebastien McGrath

Sebastien leads the engineering direction at Solnox Systems. He is driven by practical, well-built technology and the belief that thoughtful engineering can make the world cleaner, more capable, and more resilient.

Co-founder and Client Relationships Director

Pedro MacLean

Pedro leads client relationships with a focus on community, trust, and long-term impact. He is passionate about strengthening local communities through cleaner, more self-sustaining systems.

A quieter model

Solnox Systems was shaped by time spent travelling through New Zealand, where Sebastien and Pedro saw an off-grid community operating without combustion engines: no generators, petrol cars, whipper snippers, or fuel-driven machinery. That experience sharpened a shared belief that communities deserve systems that are cleaner, quieter, and built for long-term independence.

A full lifecycle view

Pedro also saw the damage caused by a lithium battery related dump fire in the Doomadgee Aboriginal Shire. We understand the benefits of lithium, and we also understand how important it is to honour the full lifecycle of batteries and tackle the challenges that come with them.
